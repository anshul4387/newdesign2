CLINICAL IMPORTANCE OF SHADVIDH UPKRAMA

Hem Raj* 1 and Manju Saini2 1 Professor Kayachikitsa, Mai Bhago Ayurvedic Medical College Muktsar/Ph.D. Scholar Sri Krishna Ayush University Kurukshetra Haryana. 2Associate Professor Govt. Ayurvedic College Patiala Punjab

ABSTRACT

Ayurveda has the ability to revolutionize the modern healthcare system and make the future medical system more sustainable. Its “Prevention is better than cure” purpose, aims to provide solutions for a healthy lifestyle to promote overall well-being. Ayurveda is a science of life with a holistic approach to health and personalized medicine. Ayurveda is a complete medical system that comprised physical, psychological, philosophical, ethical, and spiritual health.[1] Ayurveda has ability to treat many chronic diseases such as arthritis, asthma, diabetes and lifestyle disorders. Lifestyle disorders are constituted of diseases which arise because of unhealthy lifestyle. The important factors responsible for lifestyle disorders are low physical activity, unhealthy food habits, disturbed sleep patterns and incorrect body posture. This may increase the risk of developing certain critical illness in the later stage of life. The incidence of lifestyle diseases like diabetes mellitus, increased Cholesterol level, increased blood pressure and overweight associated with cardiovascular diseases is high on the rise. Ayurveda blends our lifestyle and health-oriented habits with the ancient wisdom of using Ayurvedic basic Principles, Ayurvedic medicines and natural herbs to help us lead a healthy, happy and disease-free life. Six types of treatment modalities have been mentioned in Ayurveda, known as Shadvidh-Upakram. [2] These are very beneficial in prevention and management of lifestyle diseases. In Ayurveda causes of a disease are classified into two types Santarpanjanya and Aptarpanjanya. These diseases are caused by unhealthy food habits and lifestyle. Santarpanjanya diseases are caused by taking food excess in quantity and lack of physical activities while Aptarpanjanya diseases are caused by intake of food less in quantity and more physical activities. [3] These Shadvidh-Upakram are used for prevention and cure of diseases.

KEYWORDS:

Lifestyle diseases, Aptarpanjanya vyadhi, Dosha, Santarpanjanya vyadhi, Shadvidh upakrama, Shadvidh-Upakram.

INTRODUCTION

Ayurveda effectively describes ways to prevent and manage lifestyle disorders; therefore, the world is being attracted towards its holistic approach. Ayurveda provides a better solution in the forms of proper dietary management, healthy lifestyle approaches, detoxification and rejuvenation. Maintaining health of healthy individual and treating the illness of a diseased is the main fundamental principle of Ayurveda. For applicability of this principal Acharya Charak mentioned six types of treatment protocol: langhan, brinhan, rukshan, stambhan, snehan, and swedan. [4] The whole treatment protocols of Ayurveda are included under two main principles of treatment as described by acharya vagbhaṭ. santarpaṇ and apatarpaṇ are the two main treatment modalities which are also known as langhan and bṛinhaṇ respectively.

Six types of Treatment/Shadvidh upakrama

Langhan

The treatment which brings lightness and emaciation to the body is called Langhan-the deprivation treatment, also called de-nourishing treatment. Langhan therapy is highly effective and increasingly popular in management of diseases due to overnutrition. Langhan Dravya are having properties like light, hot, sharp, non-slimy, rough, subtle, dry, fluid and hard. [5] Langhan Upakrama creates lightness in the body in case of condition suggesting dominance of kaphadosha.[6] Kaphadosha causes blockage of channels of the body which results in manifestation of diseases like as obesity, diabetes and heart diseases etc. This kaphadosha may disturb the proper functioning of Jatharagni which may lead to agnimandya. Langhan is One of the most important treatment principles. Whenever aam dosha increases in the body it disturbs the level of doshas, blocks the body channels, and leads to heaviness. In such condition langhan is best treatment whereby it helps in removing blockages of channels and brings lightness in body. Ten types of langhan therapies mentioned in ayurvedic texts. four types of elimination therapy (vaman, virechan, niruh vasti and nasya), control of thirst, exposure of the wind, exposure of the sun, intake of substance that stimulates digestion, fasting and physical exercise.[7] Langhan therapy is recommended for diseases in which there is a heaviness in the body or an obstruction in channels, such as diabetes, sinusitis, asthma, indigestion, obesity, high cholesterol and fever etc. Langhan treatments are broadly categorised in two types: 1. Shodhan (purification), which removes highly aggravated doshas by its eliminatory action from the body. 2. Shaman (palliative), which transforms dosha vaishamyata into dosha samyata by its subsiding- action in the body. In context of shodhan; Vaman is the best example for managing increased kaph dosh in tamak shwas/bronchial asthma.[8] Virechan is beneficial in amlapitt[9] similarly vasti in avaranajanya vatvyadhi[10] and shirovirechan in kaphaj shirashool. [11] Other types of langhan like pipasa, aatpa and maruta sewan in alpadosh, upavas in taruna jwar as principle of management starts with langhan.[12] Deepan pachan in aam conditions to bring it to niram state, vyayama in urustambh and obesity shows effectiveness of shaman type of langhan. [13] Benefits of Langhan therapy includes: feeling of hunger and thirst, purity of heart and mind, clearness of throat, diseases become less severe, enthusiasm increases, disappearance of lassitude.[14] Langhan digests aam dosha and increase digestive power and establishes doshas in equilibrium. Langhan leads to decrease in intensity dosha, increases agni or digestive fire and brings lightness in the body. Thirst and hunger develop well accordingly. As like Ayurveda modern science too states the theory of autophagy which resembles to langhan therapy. Autophagy is physiological process in the body that deals with cell destruction in the body, it maintains homeostasis or normal functioning by protein degradation and turnover of the destroyed cell organelle for new cell formations. During cellular stress the process of autophagy is upscaled and increased, cellular stress is caused when there is deprivation of nutrients or growth factors. Hence autophagy may come up with an alternative source of intracellular building blocks and substrates that may originate energy to enable continuous cell survival. Autophagy reduces damaged organelles, cell membranes and proteins. Failure autophagy is the main reason for accumulation of cell damage and aging.[15]

Brinhan

The term brinhan means building, nourishing or strengthening. The brinhan therapies are indicated in degenerative conditions to nourish and replenish the depleted body components. The medicines or procedures increase bulk of body components. Thus, this therapy makes body strong and firm.[16] The dravya which provide nourishment to the body is known as brinhan dravya. The nourishing substance possess properties like heavy (guru) leading to fullness, cold potency (sheet), softness (mridu), unctuousness (snigdh), thickness (bahal), gross (sthool), sliminess (pichchhil), slowness (mand), stability (sthir) and smoothness (shlakshn).[17] These drugs are having dominancy of prithvi and aap mahabhoot. This treatment is included under Santarpan chikitsa. These drugs act at the level of dhatvagni and thus helps in proper nourishment of dhatu. Those patients who are lean and thin or suffering from related disorders such as general debility, weight loss etc. are treated with the use of brinhan chikitsa. The brinhan dravya by a virtue of their properties increases the bulkiness of dhatu and body and it mainly focus on structural and functional aspect of respective dhatu and organs therefore increase the strength and weight of body. We can say that these drugs increase the absorptions of micro-nutrients at the level of agni by promoting the digestion and metabolism in the body and boost up the immunity. It also acts at the level of srotas by promoting the microcirculation and tissue perfusion along providing direct nutrition to tissue. So brinhan therapy helps to strengthen and rejuvenate the body.

Rookshan

Rookshan means drying; the treatment that causes dryness, roughness in the body is called Rookshan. The therapy by which the oily, sticky and fatty constituents of the body are dried up, which also eliminates excess mucus, fat and water from the tissues and organs of the body is known as Rookshan.[18] The drugs which make the body rough have the properties of rooksh, laghu, khar, teekshan, sthir, pichchhil, kathin and drav. [19] It can be used in kaphaj Diseases like as diabetes, heart diseases, obesity, thyroid dysfunctions, inflammatory diseases and so many. If there is dominance of kaphadosha, rookshan chikitsa should be done. The rookshan chikitsa dries the body and makes it looking thinner with absorption of all the body fluids which is not essential in the body. So many diseases in body are manifested by aam dosha caused by mandagni. This aam dosha accumulate in the body causes srotovarodh which affects the proper nutrition of next dhatu. if we apply rookshan chikitsa in such conditions, it has a role in the digestion of aam dosha. Rookshan can be done both externally and internally. External Rookshan is carried out by procedures such as Udvartan and application of various rooksh lepas whereas internal rookshan is done with administration of takra pana, rooksh anna sewan and rooksh drugs. [20]

Snehan

The treatment by which oiliness (snigdhata), liquefaction (vishyandata), softness (mardavata) and moistness (kledata) are achieved called as snehan. [21] These drugs have the properties like sukshm, sar, snigdh, pichchhil, guru, sheet, mand and mridu.[22] According to action, snehan are of 3 types
  1. Shodhan: Sneha administered prior to Shodhan is known as Shodhan Sneha. It is administered for the purpose of Dosh Utkleshan prior to vaman and virechan. [24]
  2. Shaman: Sneha administered processed with herbs that can cure diseases. In general, the sneha dosage is administered in empty stomach when person feels hungry during Ann kaal. [25]
  3. Brinhan: Small amounts of sneha given along with food for the purpose of nourishment is known as Brinhan Sneha.[26]

According to route of administration, snehan are of 2 types[27]

  1. External (Bahya) such as Abyang, Lep, Avagah, Parisekh, karnapooran, akshitarpan.
  2. Internal (Abhyantar) like Snehpan, Basti, Nasya.
Snehan karma is performed as Purvakarma in relation to various shodhan therapies. It improves the digestive power, renovates of body tissues, increase body strength, improve skin texture, delay aging process. It acts as solvent. It brings the lodged morbid and unexcreted waste products in to gastro-intestinal tract which can be further expelled out through oral or anal route by the use of shodhana chikitsa.

Swedan

It is the process by which the sweat is produced in the body by using various methods so that stiffness, heaviness and coldness of body is relieved.[28] Elimination of toxins and wastes through sweat takes place in this treatment. Properties of drugs used for swedan are ushn, teekshn, sar, snigdh, ruksh, sukshm, drav, sthir and guru. [29] Swedan is a treatment commonly practiced either as a preparatory procedure to Panchakarma therapy or as an independent intervention. It is very essential and important poorvkarm of panchkarma therapy. Swedan treatment is also recommended as a first line therapy to many musculoskeletal and neuromuscular conditions identified as vataj nanatmaj diseases in Ayurveda which are caused by Vata dosha alone. The action of sudation /fomentation is to liquify the entire toxin present in diseases area from where they come out from the body through sweating. Mainly there are two methods of Swedan, niragni represents the method where the body is warmed without getting heated directly. This method is similar to what we understand as active heat therapy. Common examples to this variety are heavy clothing, exercise, etc. on other hand, saagni swedan utilizes various methods of applying dry or moist heat directly upon the body to make it warm. This method is similar to conventional methods of passive heat therapies.[30] Swedan is well known for its relaxing and detoxifying effects.

Stambhan

The treatment that prevents mobility and the flow of body substances and fluids is known as stambhan chikitsa. [31] The drugs which are used in this treatment are called stambhan dravya. Stambhan treatment involves stopping movement of vitiated doshas. Properties of stambhan drugs are laghu, sheet, mridu, drav, slakshn, ruksh, sukshm and sthir. [32] These drugs increase the jal and prithvi mahabhoot. Pichchha vasti in rakta atiisar and avapeed nasya in nasagat raktapitt are few of the best examples of stambhan. [33] There are various drugs which can be used as a stambhak dravya like durva, lodhra, priyangu and mochras etc. These stambhan drugs have kashay rasa dominancy and act like as vasoconstrictor. This treatment is very important in stopping haemorrhage, preventing and treating blood loss, diarrhoea and other disorders like ulcerative colitis.

DISCUSSION

Shadvidh upakrama helps to balance the abnormality of tridoshas in the body. Langhan is beneficial in Kapha-Pittaj abnormalities. Brinhan act as Vaat-Pitt har. Rukshan decrease the Kapha and Pitta, Snehan is Vata-Pittahara, Swedan is Vata-Kaph-har and Stambhan eliminates the Pitta and Vata dosha. Shadvidh upakrama also act upon gurvadi gunas as per the principles of samanya vishesh. Langhan possess the laghu guna, brinhan has guru, rookshan has ruksh, snehan has snigdh, swedan has ushn and stambhan has sheet guna. This principle signifies importance of these six modalities of treatment. Shadvidh upakrama plans to act by balancing the proportion of the panchmahabhutas in the body. As brinhan acts by increasing prithvi and jal mahabhoot. Snehan increases the jal and prithvi mahabhoot, Stambhan increases the prithvi and jal, langhan increases the akash, vayu and agni mahabhoot, rukshan increases akash, vayu and agni. Swedan increases agni, vayu and akasha mahabhoot. These are responsible for the metabolic activity of the body. So Shadvidh upakrama are helpful in balancing tridosh.

CONCLUSION

Main aim of shadvidh upakrama is balancing Dosha or Dosh Saamyata. It is attained through balancing the gunas, ultimately by achieving the Panchabhouthika stability. Shadvidh upakrama works on the principle of the samanya vishesh sidhant and acts by balancing the proportion and on the basis of the combination of panchmahabhutas in the body. In brief there are main 3 action of shadvidh upakrama;
  1. Shadvidh upakrama act by balancing the proportion of the panchmahabhutas in the body
  2. Shadvidh upakrama acts on the principles of samanya vishesh of the gurvadi gunas.
  3. Shadvidh upakrama helps to balance the tridoshas in the body.
Shadvidh Upakram is used both for prevention and cure of disease.
